# Archetypes — fit map

Launch pad, not a cage. Locate the nearest archetype, take its **anti-fits** as hard eliminations and its directions as starting fuel. The subject-world (SKILL.md Step 1) always overrides this table — if a direction here doesn't tie to *this* subject, ground it or drop it.

Each archetype: **fitting directions** (with the why) and **anti-fits** (the slop-menu reaches that fight this project).

---

## Efficiency / productivity tool (task manager, notes, dev workflow)
**Fitting:** Keyboard-first density (the UI rewards mastery, like a pro tool); monospace-ledger (work as a legible record); calm-canvas (the app recedes so the work is foreground); command-surface (everything reachable, nothing decorative).
**Anti-fits:** Glassmorphism (blur fights legibility), maximalist motion (friction for a daily tool), big-hero-marketing layouts inside the app.

## Finance / fintech / trading
**Fitting:** Terminal-precision (tickers, fills, tabular figures, green/red as data not decoration); ledger-paper (trust through the vocabulary of accounting); dense-grid dashboards where number alignment is the design.
**Anti-fits:** Playful pastels, claymorphism, rounded-everything (reads as toy, kills trust), organic blobs.

## Healthcare / clinical / medical
**Fitting:** Calm-clinical (high legibility, generous space, restraint signals competence); reassurance-warm (soft neutrals, never sterile-cold); evidence-forward (clear hierarchy, data you can trust at a glance).
**Anti-fits:** Brutalism (aggression in a care context), neon/dark-hacker, gamified celebration on serious actions.

## Developer tool / API / infrastructure
**Fitting:** Docs-as-product (the terminal and code block are the hero); dark-IDE-native; monospace-systematic; diagram-driven (architecture made visible).
**Anti-fits:** Stock-photo hero of smiling teams, decorative gradients, illustration-heavy whimsy.

## Creative portfolio / design studio
**Fitting:** Editorial-asymmetric (the work is the content, type is the voice); kinetic-type (motion as craft demonstration); gallery-restraint (white space frames the work); broken-grid where the break is intentional.
**Anti-fits:** Template SaaS three-card rows, centered-everything, the slop menu applied generically (this audience reads it instantly).

## E-commerce / DTC product
**Fitting:** Product-as-hero (full-bleed photography, the object front and center); editorial-lookbook (magazine framing for lifestyle goods); tactile-detail (texture, material honesty for craft goods).
**Anti-fits:** Dense dashboards, terminal aesthetics, anything that buries the product behind chrome.

## Editorial / publication / blog / news
**Fitting:** Typographic-first (the reading experience is the design); newspaper-structure when the content is genuinely sequential/hierarchical; quiet-serif for long-form (justified by the read, not the vibe).
**Anti-fits:** Card-grid everything, app-chrome around articles, motion that interrupts reading.

## SaaS B2B / dashboard
**Fitting:** Information-architecture-forward (clarity of dense data is the win); systematic-tokens (a real design system, consistency as professionalism); focused-canvas (one primary action per screen).
**Anti-fits:** Awwwards experimental motion (friction for daily work), maximalism, hero marketing patterns inside the product.

## Consumer social / community
**Fitting:** Content-immersive (the feed/content fills the frame); personality-forward (a distinct voice the community recognizes); thumb-native mobile-first.
**Anti-fits:** Corporate-sterile B2B greys, dense data tables, formal serif gravitas.

## Kids / education / playful learning
**Fitting:** Bold-friendly (saturated, high-contrast, legible for young eyes); illustration-character-led; reward-celebratory (peak moments designed, success feels good).
**Anti-fits:** Terminal/hacker dark, fine-print density, muted luxury neutrals.

## Luxury / premium brand
**Fitting:** Restraint-as-luxury (space and precision over ornament); material-honest (real photography, considered texture); editorial-confident (the brand doesn't shout).
**Anti-fits:** The AI premium-default (beige+brass+oxblood+espresso — banned, see design-taste-frontend), busy density, neon.

## AI / ML product
**Fitting:** Capability-demonstrated (show the model working, live, not described); systematic-precise (engineering credibility); restrained-futurism (confidence over sci-fi cliché).
**Anti-fits:** AI-purple glow gradients (the #1 tell), generic "neural network" particle backgrounds, dark-mesh-with-acid-accent.

## Events / conference / launch
**Fitting:** Poster-bold (the event has a single identity, like a gig poster); date-time-place forward (the information IS the design); kinetic-announcement where the moment justifies motion.
**Anti-fits:** Quiet dashboards, dense data UI, generic SaaS templates.

## Nonprofit / public-sector / civic
**Fitting:** Trust-first clarity (accessibility-led, plain, GOV.UK-grade legibility); evidence-and-impact forward; human-photography honest.
**Anti-fits:** Experimental layouts that risk usability, dark-mode-default, decorative motion, anything that reads as marketing slick over substance.

## Music / audio / entertainment
**Fitting:** Album-art-led (cover/visual is the hero); waveform/rhythm-native (the medium's own forms); immersive-dark for playback contexts.
**Anti-fits:** Corporate B2B greys, spreadsheet density, formal serif.

## Wellness / meditation / calm
**Fitting:** Analog-warmth (soft, low-contrast, dusk palettes); breathing-space (generous, slow, motion that calms); single-focus (one gentle action).
**Anti-fits:** Dense dashboards, high-energy saturation, terminal/data aesthetics, urgent motion.

---

## When nothing fits

Derive straight from the subject-world (SKILL.md Step 1). Name the subject's materials, instruments, and vocabulary, and let directions grow from those. An archetype miss is normal — the table is a launch pad, and the subject is always the stronger source.
